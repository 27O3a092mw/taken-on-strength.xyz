Lance Corporal Arthur Alway, 127580
Year Date Detail Reference(s)
1895 October 6
  • Born in Alveston, South Gloucestershire, England [map ⧉]
  • Family
    • Father: George Alway
    • Mother: Annie Alway
 1 (p.3), 2
1897 May 26 Brother, William Henry Alway born in Alveston. 3
June 20 William was christened in Alveston. 4
1901 March 31 Arthur, 5 years old, and his family are enumerated in the England and Wales census.

They lived in Alveston. George, 28 years old, was listed as an "Agricultural Labourer", Annie was 26, and William was 3 years old. 		2
1911 April 2 The Alway family is enumerated in the 1911 England and Wales census.

Arthur, now 15, has an occupation of "milk boy on farm", his father George, 38, is listed as "cow man on farm", and brother William, 13, is listed as an "errand boy". Mother, Annie is now 36. 		5
1913 June 17-25 Arthur sailed from Avonmouth, Bristol, England [map ⧉] on the Royal George [Wikipedia ⧉], on it's first voyage back after being repaired. He was heading to Quebec City, Quebec, Canada [map ⧉]. When he arrived on June 25, 1913, he was included in a list of passengers with the following details.
  • Age: 17
  • Destination: Ingersoll, Ontario, Canada [map ⧉]
  • Intended occupation in Canada: farm labour
  • Religious denomination: Church of England
 6, 7, 8
1915 October 7 Arthur enlisted in the 71st Battalion of the Canadian Expeditionary Force (CEF) [Wikipedia ⧉] at Embro, Ontario, Canada [map ⧉] the following information comes from his enlistment (attestation) form.
  • Next of Kin: George Alway, father of Alveston, South Gloctershire, Eng. [sic]
  • He was not married
  • Confirmed that he was working as a farm labourer before enlisting
  • He had no previous military or militia experience.
  • His enlistment was witnessed by Douglas Grigg [Personnel File ⧉], another man in the 71st Battalion
  • The "certificate of magistrate" section is signed by Major Frederick Oley Burgess [Personnel File ⧉], an officer in the 71st Battalion.
 1 (p.3)
November 16 Arthur underwent his enlistment medical examination in Woodstock, Ontario, Canada [map ⧉]. From this exam we can get a bit of a physical description of him:
  • Height: 5 feet 9 inches (175.26 cm)
  • Weight: 145 lbs (65.77 kg, 10.36 stone)
  • Eye colour: brown
  • Hair colour: brown
  • Chest measurement
    • Minimum: 34 inches (86.36 cm)
    • At maximum expansion: 37 inches (93.98 cm)
  • Physical development: good
  • Small-pox marks: none
  • Vaccination marks: 2 on right arm
  • Last vaccinated: in childhood
  • Religion: Church of England
  • No marks indicating “congenital pecularities or previous disease” and no “slight defects but not sufficient to cause rejection”

The doctor who signed off on the examination appears to be Captain Charles MacKenzie MacKay [Personnel File ⧉].
After the examination his attestation paper was signed by the officer commanding the 71st Battalion, who I believe was Lieutenant Colonel Donald Matheson Sutherland [Personnel File ⧉], at that time. 		1 (pp.4,7)
1916 March 2 Received a vaccination and anti-typhoid innoculation from Captain MacKay 1 (p.7)
March 13 Received a second anti-typhoid innoculation from the medical officer 1 (p.7)
April 1 Embarked the Olympic [Wikipedia ⧉] at Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada [map ⧉] to sail to England with the 71st Battalion. 1 (p.9), 9
April 11 Disembarked the Olympic at Liverpool, England [map ⧉] 1 (p.9)
May 25 Transferred to the 73rd Battalion (Royal Highlanders of Canada) [Wikipedia ⧉] 1 (p.9)
June 9 There is a duplicate of a will made out by Arthur, that was originally signed on this day. In this will we get the following information:
  • Unit: 73rd Battalion R.H.C.
  • Next of Kin: Mrs. G. Alway (mother) of "Rudgeway Nr Bristol, England, Gloucestershire" [sic]
  • Witness 1: Lance Corporal Harold William Huntley
    • Address: 73rd Battalion R.H.C. C.E.F.
    • Occupation: Soldier
  • Witness 2: Austin Cunningham
    • Address: Huntingdon, Quebec, Canada [map ⧉]
    • Occupation: Soldier
  • Dated by an officer from I/C Estates (Link?) on January 24, 1918.
  • Contains the following typed notes:
    • "Killed in action 31-10-17"
    • "transferred from living 23-1-18 No. 127580 L/Cpl Alway, A. 42nd Bn"
 1 (p.5)
July 22 A note on his Medical History Sheet is dated for this day with "Tab/1 L7" 1 (p.7)
August 12 Began the process of travelling to France with the 73rd Battalion 1 (p.9)
August 13 Disembarked at Le Havre, France [map ⧉] 1 (p.9)
1917 January 29 There are 4 entries on the "Casualty Form - Active Service" page for this day.
  1. Report from the officer commanding 73rd Battalion, Arthur reported to field ambulance unit "sick"
  2. Report from 11th Field Ambulance that Arhtur was admitted for "I.C.T. Leg, L", which is "inflammation of the connective tissue" left leg.
  3. Report from 11th Field Ambulance that he was being transferred to 22nd Casualty Clearing Station
  4. Report from 22nd Casualty Clearing Station that Arthur was admitted "I.C.T. Leg, L"
 1 (p.9), 10
