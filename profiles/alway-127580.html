Date of Birth: October 6, 1895 in either Alveston, England or Thornbury, England1
Family: From the 1901 and 1911 England and Wales census' we are able to see that Arthur's family consisted of his father George, mother Annie, and younger brother William
Occupation: On the 1911 England and Wales census, Arthur was 15 years old, and listed as a "milk boy on farm". His father George was a "cow man", and William was listed as "errand boy". When Arthur left England in 1913 and arrived in Canada he gave his trade as "farm labour", and would indicate the same when he enlisted in the CEF in 1915.
Moved to Canada: Arthur arrived in Quebec City, Canada on board the Royal George, on June 24, 1913. He indicated that he would be heading to Ingersoll, Ontario as his destination
Canadian Expeditionary Force: Arthur enlisted in the CEF the day after his 20th birthday, October 7, 1915 in Embro, Ontario. His enlistment medical describes him as 5'9" (175.26 cm) tall, weighing 145 lbs (65.78 kg), having brown hair and eyes, and a medium complexion.

On enlistment he joined the 71st Battalion, which had been formed in August 1915. He would sail with the 71st from Halifax, Nova Scotia to Liverpool, England, on April 1, 1916 aboard the Olympic.

He was transferred to the 73rd Battalion on May 25, 1916 and travelled with that unit to Le Havre, France on August 12, 1916.

April 19, 1917 Arthur was transferred to the 42nd Battalion, and September 9 he was appointed Lance Corporal. Unfortunately on October 31, 1917 Arthur was killed in action at Passchendaele, Belgium. He is buried at Tyne Cot Cemetery southwest of Passchendaele.
