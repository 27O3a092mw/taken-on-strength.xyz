Lance Corporal Arthur Alway, 127580
Year Date Detail Reference(s)
1895 October 6
  • Born in Alveston, South Gloucestershire, England (map ⧉)
  • Family
    • Father: George Alway

 1, 2
1897 May 26 Brother, William Henry Alway born in Alveston. 3
June 20 William was christened in Alveston. 4
1901 March 31 Arthur, 5 years old, and his family are enumerated in the England and Wales census.

They lived in Alveston. George, 28 years old, was listed as an "Agricultural Labourer", Annie was 26, and William was 3 years old. 		2
1911 April 2 The Alway family is enumerated in the 1911 England and Wales census.

Arthur, now 15, has an occupation of "milk boy on farm", his father George, 38, is listed as "cow man on farm", and brother William, 13, is listed as an "errand boy". Mother, Annie is now 36. 		5
1913 June 17-25 Arthur sailed from Avonmouth, Bristol, England (map ⧉) on the Royal George, on it's first voyage back after being repaired. He was heading to Quebec City, Quebec, Canada (map ⧉). When he arrived on June 25, 1913, he was included in a list of passengers with the following details.
  • Age: 17
  • Destination: Ingersoll, Ontario, Canada (map ⧉)
  • Intended occupation in Canada: farm labour
  • Religious denomination: Church of England
