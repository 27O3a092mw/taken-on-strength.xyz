Information About Sources

There are a number of sources I use for the research I present here. A lot of the information comes from Library and Archives Canada. I'll make a table below of my common sources and notes I have on them.

Source Title (and link) Source Owner Notes
A guide to the Western Front WW1 battlefields of 1914-1918. greatwar.co.uk Just a collection of interesting information
Abbreviations used in Canadian First World War personnel records Ancestry Solutions (Not related to Ancestry.com) a handy guide to finding information in CEF personnel files, also archived here https://archive.is/ISYY0
Abbreviations used in the First World War medal index cards [PDF] The National Archives of the United Kingdom Abbreviations you may find in files.
Abbreviations used in World War I and World War II service records | naa.gov.au National Archives of Australia Abbreviations you may find in files.
Books of Remembrance Government of Canada All who die in the service of Canada are recorded in a book of remembrance; these books can be searched and copies of pages aquired.
Canadian Great War Project Great project, so much information available and transcribed. Fantastic site.
Canadian Geographical Names Database (CGNDB) Government of Canada - Natural Resources Canada This is a great source to locate historically named places in Canada. Lots of people that signed up for the CEF did so coming from tiny villages or hamlets. Some of them are really only known now by people who live in the area, or have been renamed but this database has information and location details.
Canadian Virtual War Memorial Government of Canada A database of those who have died in uniform in the service of Canada.
Common British Army acronyms and abbreviations of the First World War The Long, Long Trail Abbreviations you may find in files.
Commonwealth War Graves Commission A database of graves for those who died in war. Covers the Commonwealth nations.
David Rumsey Historical Map Collection | The Collection Cartography Associates Great source for old maps; very nice layout and a lot of data available here.
Distinguishing Patches of the CEF Originally found at Military History Books by Harold A. Skaarup but I archived it with archive.today in case it is taken down at some point Just a helpful post of unit patches in case your looking at photos
Find a Grave ancestry.com Database of cemeteries and graves. Very useful for finding information. I update records here when possible with information from CEF files. My profile at find a grave
In Search of Your Canadian Past: The Canadian County Atlas Digital Project McGill University Some very interesting information and old maps of Canada. Helpful for finding places that have had their names changed etc.
Index to WWI Trench Maps and Aerial Photographs McMaster University Library Very helful for trench maps and finding places that were given names by the Entente armies.
Old Maps Online OldMapsOnline Another good source for old maps; catalogues old maps
Prisoners of the First World War, the ICRC Archives The International Committee of the Red Cross I haven't used this source very much yet, but it's interesting. It contains data from all sides, about prisoners and requests they received to confirm prisoner of war status. Lots of different languages in these images, so keep a translator program handy if needed.
Researching Canadian Soldiers of the First World War Michael O'Leary; The Regimental Rogue Helpful guide to trench map system
WW1 Abbreviations and Military Terms - NZ Society of Genealogists New Zealand Society of Genealogists Abbreviations you may find in files.
 
Library and Archives Canada Pages
Collection Search The main catalogue search page. For WWI personnel files, click on Advanced Search, then select First World War Personnel Files from the Database field. You can then search by name, birth or enlistment province, regimental number, unit, etc.
Citing LAC Material Helpful guide put out to cite information properly.
Circumstances of Death Register This database of images shows a form that was filled out for each soldier killed in action; there are a number missing, notably forms from last names starting with "Sip" to "Z" have not survived. However a very useful resource. I don't know whether these cards have been transcribed by any other project, but it's something I am looking into doing if not.
Depot Battalions [PDF] A guide to the depot battalions of the CEF
First World War The current homepage of LAC for First World War sources; not sure how long until this is transitioned over to the new LAC site (as of: 2023-07-14)
Infantry Battalions [PDF] A guide to infantry battalions of the CEF
Military abbreviations used in service files Library and Archives Canada abbreviations page.
Reserve Battalions [PDF] A guide to reserve battalions of the CEF
 
Unrelated, but useful in this sort of thing
Names List Charts unicode.org Helpful if you want to get some characters not normally on the English keyboard, ½, ¾, №, etc.
